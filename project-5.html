Background & Problem Context

Project Introduction

Learning to See, Seeing to Learn is an interdisciplinary research and development effort to create an innovative new kind of teaching and learning resource for aquatic insect identification to support citizen science water quality biomonitoring and environmental education activities.

Thanks to Marti Louw at CMU Learning Media Design Center, I’m honored to be involved in this amazing project - macroinvertebrates.org from May 2020 to August 2020.

What are macroinvertebrates?

Macroinvertebrates are small aquatic animals and the aquatic larval stages of insects. In scientific research, macroinvertebrate identification is critical in providing high-quality data for water quality monitoring of local streams, lakes, and other ground water resources.

Problems

Macroinvertebrates.org generates around 2,000 monthly users. 20% of them access the website on a mobile device, which results in the website layout breaking on mobile and makes it difficult to take advantage of our most important feature, the zoomable high-res gigpan images.

Main Goals

The summer 2020 version of the app is an innovative open educational resource with the aim of making the task of learning to identify macroinvertebrate easier and more engaging by developing a robust mobile app.

  • Easily accessible even with low-no wifi in outdoor settings
  • Engaging for the user with interactive learning experiences through mobile interactions and gestures
  • Viewed as a tool that is scientifically reliable for experts, yet approachable for new learners

APP Features

Field Guide

The Field Guide is designed with pages that scale across the Order, Family, and Genus taxonomy levels. It is both a reference and learning material with 93 Families within 9 Orders that can be found and learned about.

ID Key

The ID Key is a complex web of different paths users can go through to identify unknown insects. Translating the ID Key into the digital space, we have created an innovative, first of its kind key that is mobile friendly and also interactively designed. Various features have been implemented in this section to guide novices in making more confident identifications.

Flashcard Practice

The Flashcard Practice helps increase learner's engagement, confidence, and accuracy in macroinvertebrate identification. This section had almost a complete overhaul from the initial designs and is expanding into a few other modes such as 'Review Look-Alikes' and 'Multiple Choice Quiz.'

Navigation: Menu & Search

Hamburger Menu

There is a lot of complexity within the app, so we created the Menu feature that would give our users more contextual information on the app and to find more micro resources and features to aid different learning goals.

Glossary

We received feedback on how our app could be more friendly for “the average joe”. With the aim of better supporting these users, we incorporated a glossary into the menu to help novices brush up different vocabulary and concepts. Content was taken from the glossary on our site but made accessible for handheld devices through the scrollable index.

Search

We also built a search function as another way to navigate and jump around the Field Guide for more expert users. This would show recent searches and find search matches at the Order and Family level.

‍MacroCollection

A beautiful visual on the website is the full collection of insects in one shot. However, in PocketMacros’ field guide, you can only view a couple of them at a time at the Family level. We built MacroCollection as a way for users to index through all the insects so they can quickly see everything that the collection has to offer.
