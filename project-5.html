Background & Problem Context

Project Introduction

Learning to See, Seeing to Learn is a an interdisciplinary research and development initiative that aims to create a cutting-edge educational resource for aquatic insect identification, with a focus on supporting citizen science water quality biomonitoring and environmental education activities.

Thanks to Marti Louw at CMU Learning Media Design Center, I’m honored to be involved in this amazing project - macroinvertebrates.org from May 2020 to August 2020.

What are macroinvertebrates?

Macroinvertebrates, which are small aquatic animals and the larval stages of insects, play a crucial role in scientific research by providing valuable data for water quality monitoring in local streams, lakes, and other groundwater resources. Accurate identification of macroinvertebrates is essential for obtaining high-quality monitoring data.

Problems

The website Macroinvertebrates.org currently receives approximately 2,000 monthly users, with 20% of them accessing the site on a mobile device. However, the current layout of the website does not adapt well to mobile screens, causing issues with the display of our key feature, the zoomable high-resolution gigpan images, and making it challenging for users to take full advantage of this important functionality.

Main Goals

The main goals of the summer 2020 version of the app are to create an innovative open educational resource that makes learning to identify macroinvertebrates easier and more engaging.

This will be achieved by developing a robust mobile app:

  • Easily accessible even with low-no wifi in outdoor settings
  • Engaging for the user with interactive learning experiences through mobile interactions and gestures
  • Viewed as a tool that is scientifically reliable for experts, yet approachable for new learners

APP Features

Field Guide

A comprehensive reference and learning tool with scalable pages covering taxonomy levels, allowing users to explore and learn about 93 Families within 9 Orders of macroinvertebrates.

ID Key

An interactive and mobile-friendly identification tool that guides users through a complex web of paths to confidently identify unknown insects, enhancing the identification process.

Flashcard Practice

Engaging practice mode with different modes such as 'Review Look-Alikes' and 'Multiple Choice Quiz' to increase learner engagement, confidence, and accuracy in macroinvertebrate identification.

Navigation: Menu & Search

Hamburger Menu

There is a lot of complexity within the app, so we created the Menu feature that would give our users more contextual information on the app and to find more micro resources and features to aid different learning goals.

Glossary

We received feedback on how our app could be more friendly for “the average joe”. With the aim of better supporting these users, we incorporated a glossary into the menu to help novices brush up different vocabulary and concepts. Content was taken from the glossary on our site but made accessible for handheld devices through the scrollable index.

Search

We also built a search function as another way to navigate and jump around the Field Guide for more expert users. This would show recent searches and find search matches at the Order and Family level.

‍MacroCollection

A beautiful visual on the website is the full collection of insects in one shot. However, in PocketMacros’ field guide, you can only view a couple of them at a time at the Family level. We built MacroCollection as a way for users to index through all the insects so they can quickly see everything that the collection has to offer.

Implementation
