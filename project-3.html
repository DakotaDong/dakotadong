Problem Space

What is Social and Emotional Learning (SEL)?

According to the Collaborative for Academic, Social, and Emotional Learning (CASEL), “Social and emotional learning (SEL) is the process through which children and adults understand and manage emotions, set and achieve positive goals, feel and show empathy for others, establish and maintain positive relationships, and make responsible decisions.”

Why SEL?

A growing body of research indicates educators should aim to meet the needs of the Whole Child by integrating academic, cognitive, social and emotional instruction.

"SEL is the foundation, the heartbeat of the classroom.

Connecting everyone and making them feel safe and secure before getting to academics.”

-- News and Features from the National Education Association by Tim Walker

In the 2019 HMH Educator Confidence Report, nearly all of the teachers and administrators surveyed by HMH said they felt it’s essential to address the social and emotional needs of students. This is all happening alongside a renewed focus on mental health, behavioral outcomes, and classroom climate in K-12 education.

SEL is not going well at home and school…

During our interviews and research, we got a lot of complaints from students, parents and teachers.

For students, they have plenty of hours at home, but they don’t know how to manage their days. They wanna have a person to talk with, to be listened to, but sometimes their parents just cannot understand them.

“My parents can’t understand me sometimes, and I want to be listened to.”

“I don’t know how to manage my days.”

-- quotes from student interviews

For parents, some of them don’t know how to deal with kids. they may over-control their kids and arrange everything for them. Or they may ignore home education, and give kids all of the freedom. Overall, we found conflicts happen a lot at home.

“I used to be busy with my work, and spent little time with my kid. Now, I don’t know how to communicate with him, and he seems unwilling to talk with me.”

-- quotes from parent interviews

For teachers, it’s impossible to observe every student at the same time so they cannot track every student's social and emotional status, and the lack of student data makes it harder to assess student social emotional abilities.

“It’s hard to keep track of every student’s status. Online teaching makes it even harder.”

-- quotes from teacher interviews

Solution

Double Diamond Design Approach

To shape our digital intervention for elementary-aged students, we adopted a Double Diamond Design approach. Our process began with extensive exploratory research, encompassing academic papers, interviews, and contextual research methods. Through synthesis and convergence, we derived three key insights, leading us towards self-awareness and self-management.

Utilizing the insights gained in the initial research phase, we developed user journeys, personas, and representations of the challenges faced by different stakeholders within the school community. This allowed us to understand the obstacles encountered during the implementation of SEL in both classroom and home settings. Subsequently, we embarked on an ideation phase, generating over 20 ideas and refining them based on user testing sessions, consolidating user needs.

Through 3 iterative cycles, we designed, prototyped, and validated the feasibility of our product, "Eddie: Your SEL Buddy." This involved collaborating with 3rd-5th grade students, parents, and teachers. Additionally, we delved into research literature on motivation and assessment to inform our curriculum taxonomy, prompt/assessment/badge design.

Ideation

During our presentation to HMH stakeholders, we showcased 9 carefully chosen and promising ideas from a pool of over 30 storyboards. Together, we evaluated the feasibility and potential impact of each idea. After a voting process, Guided Diary, now known as Eddie, emerged as the preferred solution.

Eddie stands out due to its immense potential for horizontal integration across core curricula such as science, math, ELA, and social studies. Moreover, its adaptable nature enables seamless vertical integration, catering to students across different grade levels.

Content Design

Taxonomy System

To inform our taxonomy design, we turned to the CASEL framework for guidance. Through the framework's breakdown of each SEL competency, we identified the sub-competencies of self-awareness and self-management as our focal points for supporting the development of 3rd-5th grade students.

In our exploration, we also examined the work of Panorama Education and ACT Tessera. These resources provided valuable insights on how to effectively prompt students to reflect on their own behaviors. Inspired by Panorama Education's open questionnaire, which consisted of research-backed questions, we envisioned incorporating daily prompts into Eddie.

To ensure alignment with our goals, we compared the insights gathered from our assessment research with the definitions of self-awareness and self-management. As a result, we determined that targeting six specific SEL sub-competencies would be the most effective approach.

Prompt Design

We created a set of prompts for each SEL sub-competency. Each prompt is accompanied by scientifically supported activities and helpful strategies, empowering students to navigate challenges they may encounter in their lives. As they engage with these activities (a two-week period), students earn badges as a recognition of their skill development.

Badge Design

In our design, we recognize the power of digital badges as indicators of accomplishment and skills that can be displayed, accessed, and verified online. Drawing from literature on badges and their use in social networks and professional learning networks, we have integrated a badge system proven to motivate users to engage more actively.

Within the learner's profile, our badge system represents a significant breakthrough across different SEL levels. It consists of two types of badges based on SEL taxonomy: frequency badges and mastery badges.

  • Frequency badges are awarded to learners who actively interact with Eddie. These badges incentivize consistent check-ins, prompt exploration, and activity participation. They encourage learners to continually engage with the app and explore its various offerings.
  • Mastery badges are earned by learners who demonstrate progress in specific SEL skills. These badges reflect different levels of mastery, with learners assessed based on the data they input during their daily check-ins with their buddy.

Key Features

Buddies

Within our app, we offer three SEL buddies, each accompanied by a set of voice-out lines. These lines are delivered to students as they engage with prompts and activities. Our research revealed that students are more inclined to open up and form a connection with these buddies, viewing them as trusted friends.

Goal Setting

Once students have set a goal, Eddie prompts them with activities and tasks aligned with the specific competency they have chosen. These prompts span a two-week period, providing continuous support and guidance as students work towards their goal.

See My Progress

Our scrollable dashboard incorporates a feature that enables students to monitor their progress in each SEL sub-competency. To ensure clarity and understanding, the definition of each sub-competency is displayed, giving students a clear sense of what they are striving for.

As students earn badges for their achievements, their progress is meticulously recorded. Throughout their SEL journey, their buddy provides uplifting and positive feedback, fostering motivation and encouragement.

My Toolbox

In Eddie, we provide students with a variety of activities that align with the specific SEL competency relevant to their current page. To encourage regular practice, students receive a daily prompt from the toolbox when they log into Eddie. These prompts are designed to help students actively practice their SEL skills and make progress on their SEL journey.

Badges

Students are awarded with badges based on their frequency of app usage and the skills they acquire throughout their journey. These digital badges serve as visible markers of accomplishment and mastery, which can be proudly displayed, accessed, and verified online.

“Gamification would be a solution to alleviate the incentive problem related to maintaining consistent engagement in communities in which users participate voluntarily; badges motivate users to participate more.”

-- Li, Huang and Cavusoglu (2012)

Development

From a technical aspect,

  • Front-end: React framework
  • Back-end: NodeJS, ExpressJS
  • Database: MongoDB

Our development consists of two sides: server side and client side.

  • On the server side, I used handlers, middleware, and routes to store and handle user information and message information that was sent by users so that our product can allow users to signup/in and view their SEL growth. I used Mongoose to manage relationships between data, provide schema validation, and translate between objects in code and the representation of those objects in MongoDB; bcrypt module for user password hashing; jsonwebtoken module which can create JSON Web Tokens to mark users as logged in.
  • On the client side, apart from SEL buddy selection, I created prompts and relevant SEL activities and responses for 3 scenarios. I used react-router-dom module for web page switch and react-redux to manage user states.
