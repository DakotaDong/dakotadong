Background & Problem Context

What is Social and Emotional Learning (SEL)?

According to the Collaborative for Academic, Social, and Emotional Learning (CASEL), “Social and emotional learning (SEL) is the process through which children and adults understand and manage emotions, set and achieve positive goals, feel and show empathy for others, establish and maintain positive relationships, and make responsible decisions.”

Why SEL?

A growing body of research indicates educators should aim to meet the needs of the Whole Child by integrating academic, cognitive, social and emotional instruction.

"SEL is the foundation, the heartbeat of the classroom.

Connecting everyone and making them feel safe and secure before getting to academics.”

-- News and Features from the National Education Association by Tim Walker

In the 2019 HMH Educator Confidence Report, nearly all of the teachers and administrators surveyed by HMH said they felt it’s essential to address the social and emotional needs of students. This is all happening alongside a renewed focus on mental health, behavioral outcomes, and classroom climate in K-12 education.

SEL is not going well at home and school…

During our interviews and research, we got a lot of complaints from students, parents and teachers.

For students, they have plenty of hours at home, but they don’t know how to manage their days. They wanna have a person to talk with, to be listened to, but sometimes their parents just cannot understand them.

“My parents can’t understand me sometimes, and I want to be listened to.”

“I don’t know how to manage my days.”

-- quotes from student interviews

For parents, some of them don’t know how to deal with kids. they may over-control their kids and arrange everything for them. Or they may ignore home education, and give kids all of the freedom. Overall, we found conflicts happen a lot at home.

“I used to be busy with my work, and spent little time with my kid. Now, I don’t know how to communicate with him, and he seems unwilling to talk with me.”

-- quotes from parent interviews

For teachers, it’s impossible to observe every student at the same time so they cannot track every student's social and emotional status, and the lack of student data makes it harder to assess student social emotional abilities.

“It’s hard to keep track of every student’s status. Online teaching makes it even harder.”

-- quotes from teacher interviews

Solution

Ideation

Taking into account insights from our research, we generated 30+ storyboards, each of which focused on different aspects of the problem space. We tested the storyboards with 7 parents and their kids to validate what we thought were our intended users’ underlying needs.

We then selected 9 ideas based on the results of interviews and synthesis, and presented these ideas to stakeholders at HMH. We took a voke together based on feasibility and potential impact of our proposed solutions, and Guided Diary (now Eddie) wins. This solution has the most potential to be integrated horizontally across core curricula (science, math, ELA, social studies) and vertically through grades.

Meet Eddie: Your SEL Buddy!

We created Eddie with the following principles in mind as we hope to promote mutual understanding between students, their parents, and teachers.

Key Features

Buddies

We provide three SEL buddies, and each character is tied to a series of voice-out lines which are sent to the students as they complete prompts and activities in the app. We found that students are more willing to open up to their buddies and connect with them like friends.

Goal Setting

After students set a goal, Eddie issues prompts to students related to the competency the student selected over the course of two weeks.

See My Progress

The scrollable dashboard includes a way for students to track their progress in each SEL sub competency. The sub competency’s definition is displayed so students know what they’re working toward.

As students achieve badges, their progress is recorded. Their buddy gives them encouraging positive feedback as they continue in their SEL journey, and the toolbox is provided to help students improve their skills.

My Toolbox

Eddie also provides multiple activities which target the SEL competency corresponding to the page they’re on. Every day that students log into Eddie, they get a new prompt from the toolbox to practice the SEL skills.

Badges

Students are awarded badges based on how often they log into the app and the skills they master along the way. A digital badge is an indicator of accomplishment or skill that can be displayed, accessed, and verified online.

“Gamification would be a solution to alleviate the incentive problem related to maintaining consistent engagement in communities in which users participate voluntarily; badges motivate users to participate more.”

-- Li, Huang and Cavusoglu (2012)

Design Rationale

Overview

In order to determine the best way to frame our digital intervention for elementary-aged students, we employed a Double Diamond Design approach.

We started broadly on exploratory research, referring to papers, interviews and contextual research methods. And then we came to synthesize and converge to self-awareness, generating three main insights.

Based on insights obtained during the initial research phase, we created user journeys, personas, and representations of the challenges faced by each primary stakeholder in the school community when learning or implementing SEL in the classroom and at home. After that, we diverged to ideate more than 20 ideas, and came up with several insights after the user testing session to consolidate the needs.

We designed, prototyped, and validated the feasibility of the Eddie: Your SEL Buddy over 3 iterations with 3rd-5th grade students, parents, and teachers. We also read research literature on motivation and assessment, which informed our curriculum taxonomy and prompts, assessment, and badge design.

Content Design

Taxonomy System

We looked into the CASEL framework to help guide our taxonomy design. Based on CASEL’s breakdown of each SEL competency, we located the sub competencies, self-awareness and self-management, that we wanted to help 3rd-5th grade students develop.

CASEL describes self-awareness as “the ability to accurately recognize one’s own emotions, thoughts, and values and how they influence behavior. The ability to accurately assess one's strengths and limitations, with a well-grounded sense of confidence, optimism, and a growth mindset”. It includes sub competencies like identifying emotions, accurating self-perception, recognizing strength, self-confidence, and self-efficacy.

CASEL describes self-management as “the ability to successfully regulate one’s emotions, thoughts, and behaviors in different situations - effectively managing stress, controlling impulses, and motivating oneself. The ability to set and work toward personal and academic goals.” It includes impulse control, stress management, self-discipline, self-motivation, goal setting, and organization skills.

We then investigated work done by Panorama Education and ACT Tessera. We learned more about how to ask students to reflect on their own behaviors. Panorama Education drafted an open questionnaire with a set of research-backed questions for students to answer, aligning with our idea for daily prompts in Eddie.

We compared insights from our assessment research to self-awareness and self-management definitions and decided to target six SEL sub competencies.

Prompt Design

We designed a set of prompts under each SEL sub competency. For each one, we developed a set of scientifically supported activities and helpful strategies that come after the prompt. By practicing these activities, students learn how to deal with challenges that may arise in their lives while earning badges for practicing their skills.

When a student first logs into Eddie, they are prompted to set a time for a daily check-in. Every day at this time, students will get a pop-up window from their SEL buddy, saying it’s time to take a reflection. We found that every student we spoke to would regularly use the app immediately after school or just before school ends to reflect on their day.

If students don’t set a learning goal, Eddie pushes a random daily prompt to the students. Prompts corresponding to students’ least-developed skills are prioritized.

After students set a goal for a certain sub competency, Eddie prioritizes new prompts and activities to share with students. After a two week period, students earn a badge.

Badge Design

A digital badge is an indicator of accomplishment or skill that can be displayed, accessed, and verified online. According to literature about badges and their use in social networks and professional learning networks, badges are proven to motivate users to participate more.

In our design, the badge system is embedded in the learner’s profile as a breakthrough in different SEL levels. Based on SEL taxonomy, there are two types of badge systems, one based on frequency and another based on skill mastery.

  • Frequency badges are awarded to learners who interact with Eddie actively. They encourage learners to consistently check in, unlock different prompts, and try different activities.
  • Mastery badges are awarded to learners who show progress in a certain SEL skill. There are different levels of mastery and learners are assessed based on the data they input in their daily check-in with their buddy.

Development

From a technical aspect,

  • Front-end: React framework
  • Back-end: NodeJS, ExpressJS
  • Database: MongoDB

Our development consists of two sides: server side and client side.

  • On the server side, I used handlers, middleware, and routes to store and handle user information and message information that was sent by users so that our product can allow users to signup/in and view their SEL growth. I used Mongoose to manage relationships between data, provide schema validation, and translate between objects in code and the representation of those objects in MongoDB; bcrypt module for user password hashing; jsonwebtoken module which can create JSON Web Tokens to mark users as logged in.
  • On the client side, apart from SEL buddy selection, I created prompts and relevant SEL activities and responses for 3 scenarios. I used react-router-dom module for web page switch and react-redux to manage user states.
