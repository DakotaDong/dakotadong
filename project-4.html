Overview

ROLE: Learning Engineer

DURATION: Jan 2018 - Jun 2019

CONTRIBUTION:

  • Planned and conducted 50+ semi-structured interviews of teachers and school administrators to identify problems of current project-based courses
  • Worked with Subject Matter Experts to develop learning materials, learning activities, and assessments for high school students and explicit handbooks for teachers to refer to
  • Created visualized student reports with Tableau for teachers and school administrators to easily keep track of student learning and provide them with timely and personalized help

Problem

From the conversion with high school teachers and school administrators, I found current project-based courses have lots of problems...

"Sometimes I don’t know when I should give students instructional intervention."

"I wanna a scientific standard to assess my students’ learning."

"I don't have lots of teaching experience in project-based courses, and I really wanna get an explicit instructor handbook which I can refer to."

"I think tracking student learning is very important, but It’s hard to do just by observation. I wanna a tool to help me record learning and show learning data clearly."

"As a school administrator, I’d like to monitor each class performance and get student reports by classes."

Solution

Engineering Design Process (EDP) Course

Based on the insights from interviews, I chose to develop the Engineering Design Process (EDP) course, and connect it with the school's learning platform in order to help teachers and administrators track student learning.

Specifically, my solution consists of two parts:

  • HeartRate Box Course for high school students to equip them with EDP thinking abilities and application skills, and explicit handbook for teachers to refer to.
  • The design of visualized student reports with Tableau, which is embedded in the elearning platform, so that teachers and school administrators can easily keep track of student learning and provide them with timely and personalized help.

Why EDP?

EDP with real-world challenges is good to enhance open-ended problem solving skills, which is essential to high school students.

Project Context

Course Type

High school elective course, Engineering Design Domain, covering interdiscipline knowledge such as math, physics and biology. This project focuses on the last 10 units design of the course.

Learner

High school students who have already known the concepts of engineering, and used WonderBits Learning Kit before.

Community

Composed of students with different learning preferences and prior knowledge. Divide the diverse community into several small ability complementary groups, and provide them with suitable guidance and rich resources.

Belonging and Norms

Same project goal and self-made assessment would make them feel that they are part of the big family and are in charge of their own projects. The encouragement, feedback and guidance from the instructor would make learners feel their projects and themselves are valued.

Learner Profile

From the further interviews with learners, I analyzed the learner from 3 aspects: developmental level, prior experience and individual differences, which could help me better understand learners and then maximize the continuity between learners' goals and instructional goals.

Developmental Level

  • Age: 15 - 18
  • Have relatively strong processing abilities (e.g., given a real-world case, students can identify and summarize the challenge and solutions of the case generally)
  • In a rapid physical and social development stage

Prior Experience

  • Have basic interdiscipline knowledge covered in Engineering Design Course (e.g., math, physics and biology; can use computer, but can or cannot do programming)
  • Have basic knowledge about: functions of key modules of WonderBits learning kit and what engineering is
  • Have some project-based learning experience before, but have not worked in engineering design process

Individual Differences

  • Have different prior knowledge and interests on engineering related disciplines
  • Have different collaboration skills and working preferences

How can I assess learning?

Assessment is a tool to

“guide decisions about what content needs to be emphasized

versus that which is not really essential”

rather than

a standard to judge students.

In order to design an efficient assessment that would exhibit information indicating "students' evidence of meeting desired results", my assessment focuses on the process of learning rather than the outcome. The course includes 6 performance tasks: Key questions about EDP, 2 Case studies, one mid-project presentation, one final presentation and self-assessment by index card after each class.

  • Self-assessment by index card will be conducted after each class.
  • Most feedback will be provided before the next class. The brief feedback of the presentation will be first provided instantly after the in-class presentation.

How can I choose instructional activities?

Creating an environment for students

to struggle with their own thinking,

to express their ideas to others,

and to have their ideas challenged

can significantly improve students’ understanding of key topics

Guided by goal, assessment and instructional principles, I designed the learner-centered instruction and established class norms as well.

Instructional Principles Behind These Activities

  • The best way to create interest is to render it worth knowing. Organizing work around provocative questions and challenging problems would be helpful to provoke sustained engagement in learners, activate learners’ prior knowledge, and inform the instructor about learners’ current knowledge level.
  • The best opening questions (or problems) relate more to very particular tasks such as case studies that can naturally arise after students have had sufficient experience with the issues.
  • Lectures can emphasize links within/between concepts and between processes and the contexts of their use. Besides, instructional videos provided after the lecture can help students with relatively lower knowledge levels better understand different functions of various modules.
  • The sharing and discussion are about “ideas, issues, or problems” which can create a connection between the conceptual knowledge and the task that students did, in turn endowing the act of completing a task with deeper meaning than merely handling in an assignment.

HeartRate Box at School

"HeartRate Box" Engineering Design Process Course is applied to more than 30 high schools, which made me win the first prize in “iTeach” National College Student Digital Education Application Innovation Competition on 12/02/2018.

Also, the course content and assessment has been integrated into the school's e-learning platform, and teachers and school administrators can download different versions of student reports directly. The report for teachers will focus on the comparisons of student learning and the learning journey of every student. While the report for school administrators will be on a general level, for example, showing the comparisons of different classes.
